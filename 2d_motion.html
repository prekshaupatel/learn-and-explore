Equations of 2-D Motion

To understand motion in 2 dimension, we can analyze the motion along the x-axis and motion along the y-axis.

Motion along the x-axis

The motion along the x-axis is assumed to be non-accelerated.

  • vx is the velocity along the x-axis
  • dx is the displacement along the x-axis
  • t is time travelled
From our understanding of non-accelerated motion, we know the relation between vx, dx, and t can be expressed as: vx =
dx/t

Note: ux = vx, since the acceleration is 0, i.e, velocity remains constant in the absence of acceleration.

Motion along the y-axis

The motion along the y-axis is accelerated.

  • uy is the initial velocity along the y-axis
  • vy is the final velocity along the y-axis
  • dy is the displacement along the y-axis
  • a is the acceleration along the y-axis
  • t is time travelled
From our understanding of accelerated 1-dimensional motion, we know the relation between uy, vy, dy, a, and t. The relationship can be expressed as the following set of equations:

Combining the motion along both axis

The first variable that combines the motion along the x-axis and the y-axis is time (t).

Additionally, given the initial velocity (u) and the initial angle (θ), we can split it into its components to obtain ux and uy.

ux = vx = u cos θ
uy = u sin θ
